## A New Moral Foundation

Maharlikanism proposes a constitutional revision that embeds **virtues** as the foundation of governance — not merely rules, but a living moral code rooted in the pre-colonial spirit of *Bayani*, *Bayanihan*, and *Kagalingan*.

### The Core Virtues

1. **Katotohanan** (Truth) — Governance grounded in fact, not propaganda
2. **Katarungan** (Justice) — Equal protection and fair systems for all
3. **Kabutihan** (Goodness) — Policies that actively promote human flourishing
4. **Kagandahang-loob** (Benevolence) — Leadership as service, not power

### Why Constitutional Virtues?

The current Philippine Constitution, like most colonial-era documents, focuses on *limiting* government rather than *inspiring* it. Maharlikanism argues that a truly Maharlikan constitution should articulate *positive aspirations* — a vision of the society we are building toward.

> "The nation that forgets its virtues forgets itself."

This is not merely philosophical. Countries with virtue-embedded legal systems — from Singapore's meritocracy to Bhutan's Gross National Happiness framework — have demonstrated that values-driven governance produces measurable improvements in social cohesion, economic performance, and citizen satisfaction.

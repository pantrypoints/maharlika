## The Case for Federalism

The Philippines is one of the most geographically fragmented nations in Southeast Asia — over 7,600 islands, hundreds of languages, and dozens of distinct cultural regions. Yet it is governed as a unitary state from Manila.

Maharlikanism proposes a **Federal Republic of Maharlika** with a **Bicameral Parliament** replacing the current presidential system.

### The Federal Structure

- **Federal States** based on natural cultural-geographic regions (Ilocos, Visayas, Mindanao, etc.)
- Each state with its own **State Parliament** and elected governor
- A national **Federal Parliament** with two chambers:
  - *Lower House*: proportional representation by population
  - *Upper House*: equal representation per state (Senate equivalent)

### Why Parliamentary?

Parliamentary systems are more accountable than presidential systems because:
1. The executive *comes from* the legislature and can be removed by a vote of no confidence
2. Coalition governments require negotiation and consensus
3. Policy implementation is faster when legislature and executive are aligned
